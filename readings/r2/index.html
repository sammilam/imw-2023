Reading 2: What Screens Want
What is the thesis of this article?

Frank Chimero's "What Screens Want" emphasizes our creative ability to create abstraction, responsiveness, and interactions beyond real-world objects on the web. As an ever-growing society, Chimero spoke about the attitudes toward what is real. When software imitates the visual and function of notepads, calendars, and paper, they become accepted as they are more efficient. Chimero stated, "we no longer buy the thing we want, we buy the shape of the thing we prefer," in which associations tie strongly to the visual and use of the items. What screens do is change and have a multitude of affordances. Chimero answers his question that screens should want what we want. The web is fully capable of becoming responsive and dynamic in its' potential to display movement compared to print on a paper sheet. Humans should not adapt to technology but to produce them in many ways we desire. Chimero emphasizes that there is not a correct approach to web creation. Instead, we should build it for community engagement, creativity, and transparency.

Where do you stand with the two ideological camps: flat and skeuo?

I may have mixed emotions about both flat and skeuo in specific circumstances. I tie skeuo design closely to nostalgia because of its popularity at the beginning of technology. Although I do not hold against either ideological camp and am open to options, I stand closely to flat design due to its simplistic nature and functionality. I find a satisfying sensation when I see "clean" and "modern," which is flat design's overall aesthetic. Abstraction may be simpler to understand, yet harmful to be the overall representation of the item we refer to. Chimero exemplifies distortion, where he forgot the Earth is a sphere because of a relationship between a digital map and the world. I believe there are certain conditions to use skeuo or flat design in effectively communicating accurate and dynamic information. Overall, I agree with Chimero that design strategies depend on their context and how we want to display content. As for where I stand, I typically strive toward flat design based on what I notice in current trends and how I experience instinctual approaches when encountering interfaces.

What is a zoopraxiscope and how does it relate to web and interaction design? Find another example from filmmaking or another medium that has inspired digital design.

Eadweard Muybridge's Zoopraxiscope is a bottle movement device that creates movement by setting time when printing high-speed photographs in front of a light. It is one of the origins of screens, using travelling light and motion. It presents dynamic and immersive experiences through continuous movement and storytelling from beginning to end. I believe games also inspire digital design in approaches to how the user can interact with the web. By nature, games convey success or failure and offer numerous options to play. It carries the intrinsic value of users by intriguing curiosity and enjoyment by enforcing rules or challenges for the audience to overcome. I believe these aspects are carried in digital design to introduce content and allow the users a choice of direction and pace when exploring interfaces. For instance, users can accumulate rewarding points each time they complete a book, enabling them to make additional purchases from the store. Gamification in interfaces maintains engagement and immersion within a user's experience.
