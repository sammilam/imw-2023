Playing Jams Process

Research

At the beginning of the research, I analyzed the different utilizations of the function "click" in diverse interfaces. Normally, they help users navigate the website with buttons to reveal more information. This area. na channel filled with resources inspired me to use sound and click to trigger artwork.

Concept

I took inspiration from objects, as inspiration, to sort out functions that give an output. For example, I thought of the clock and that clicking would trigger a ticking noise. However, I settled with a boombox, which also emits sounds, but I wanted to visualize it with circles. My initial drawing on Figma involved too many details, making it challenging for the user to find what to interact with. I decided to simplify my design and mainly focus on the functionality of the interaction.

Interaction

I wanted to visualize the concept of sound with sound waves of circles from clicking on the yellow speakers. Although it was difficult, I decided to outline the steps necessary to reach this outcome. First, I needed to find out how to create this ripple effect. I searched for YouTube videos and found "CSS Ripple Effect Animation" by csPoint. The tutorial helped me understand how to animate with time, infinite, and linear. From there, I incorporated JavaScript that listens to the user click on the yellow speakers, which triggers the ripples. It creates a new div with the class waves to display the sound waves rippling out. After 3 seconds, the variable newWave would remove the waves.

Music

Interaction For music, I created two arrays to display the music and the name. I used a variable (currentSong) to keep track of the music numbers as I put three in an array. The song index aligns with the display names, which works well for assigning the correct song and name. When the user clicks on the yellow rectangle, it adds 1 to currentSong variable that switches the array to the next song.
