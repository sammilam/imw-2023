THEORIZING THE WEB 2018 | Poster Process

Initiation

When deciding which content packages I found interesting, I searched for words that may evoke a feeling to determine the overall experience. The environment and the web stood out to me as I could play with an earthly tone or a technological approach. In the end, I chose "Theorizing the Web 2018" as I wanted to represent the interrelationship of the web and people through interactions within the poster. First, I created a mood board to find compelling layouts, hierarchy, and approaches to deliver the content's voice through design.

Sketching

I looked further into the organization's website and how they designed posters for their event. The canvas filled with imagery and emphasized words "Theorizing" and "2018" gave me a sense of direction in what to prioritize in the poster. I drew sketches of possible layouts that play with layering or scale. For the term 'theorizing,' I imagine a scenario of uncovering the unknown. I wanted to play with fluctuating colours when hovering over the letters to allow interaction and discovery. Moreover, I chose a pixel monospace to display technology and sans-serif to have a human feel.

Figma Implementation

When designing on Figma, I wanted to illustrate two pages. One page is the landing, while the second presents all the information and calls to action. Since the poster is solely typographical, I decided to use asterisks and slashes to add texture and detail, filling the space with a computing aesthetic.

Exploration

From black & white to colour, I found this transition intimidating. I felt the white space around the text made the overall poster look empty and flat. As I shifted towards a dark-mode approach, I believe it best depicts technology as I can take advantage of glowing effects (to represent computer screens, etc). On the last board, I made gridlines to help myself code the flexbox layout and understand where to place elements.

Code

I experimented a lot with interactions using over and animation delay to introduce information and allow a playful aspect to the poster. I felt it brought a 2D object to life using glow, a cursor trail, scale, and transitions. When scrolling down to the second page, I wanted to try parallax scrolling using CSS.
